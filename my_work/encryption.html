Chaos: A Mathematical Adventure in Encryption


I decided that I wanted to design intelligent systems through ML, NNs and signal processing. In order to lay the foundation of solely using neural networks that can serve a purpose, I decided to integrate NNs with fundamental mathematics. For my bachelor’s minor thesis, realizing that security is an important parameter in any comprehensive system, I took cryptography as an elective during my undergradute course.

Shortly after, my interest moved into vacuum tube amplifiers. A hi-fidelity amplifier without any regular transistors (or an IC chip, for that matter) intrigued me. Solely consisting of capacitors, inductors, resistors and vacuum tubes, an old Magnavox-inspired tube amplifier was brought to life.

I had had many trial runs. One of the fails was recorded below in a video. The error occured because of wrong wiring, and out of phase feedback.


I won’t lie, it was a frustrating process to get this to work. I got lucky with my transformer manufacturer; I’d say they make some of the best transformers in the country. It took a few weeks for the tubes that I’d ordered from Russia to show up. I owe the opportunity of this project to my landlord, as he funded the entirety of the project. I agreed to build it for him.


It sounded marvelous. Analog signals never fascinated me more as Sabrina Claudio’s voice poured through the speakers. Alas, a 600V beauty of an amplifier! I went on to build and sell 3 more.
